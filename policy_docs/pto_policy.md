# Paid Time Off (PTO) Policy

**Document ID:** HR-PTO-001
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2026
**Applies To:** All full-time employees

## 1. Accrual

Full-time employees accrue PTO at a rate of 1.25 days per month worked, equal to 15 days per calendar year. Part-time employees accrue PTO on a pro-rated basis according to their scheduled hours. PTO accrual begins on an employee's first day of employment.

## 2. Carryover

Employees may carry over up to 5 unused PTO days into the following calendar year. Any PTO balance above 5 days that is not used by December 31 will be forfeited, except where prohibited by state law.

## 3. Requesting PTO

PTO requests must be submitted through the HR portal at least 5 business days in advance for requests of 3 days or fewer, and at least 15 business days in advance for requests of 4 or more consecutive days. Manager approval is required for all PTO requests.

## 4. Unplanned Absences

Employees who are unable to work due to illness or emergency should notify their manager as soon as reasonably possible, ideally before the start of the scheduled workday. Unplanned PTO does not require advance manager approval but must still be logged in the HR portal within 2 business days of the employee's return.

## 5. PTO Payout on Separation

Upon voluntary or involuntary separation, employees will be paid out for any accrued and unused PTO, in accordance with applicable state law. PTO payout is calculated at the employee's base salary rate at the time of separation.

## 6. Holidays

The company observes 9 paid company holidays annually, in addition to PTO. Holiday schedules are published each December for the following calendar year.
