# Expense Reimbursement Policy

**Document ID:** HR-EXP-003
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2026
**Applies To:** All employees

## 1. Eligible Expenses

Employees may seek reimbursement for reasonable, business-related expenses including travel (airfare, lodging, ground transportation), client meals, approved software/tools, professional development, and the remote work home office stipend described in the Remote Work Policy.

## 2. Submission Process and Deadlines

All expense reports must be submitted through the expense management portal within 30 calendar days of the expense being incurred. Expenses submitted after 30 days require director-level approval and may be denied at the company's discretion. Original itemized receipts are required for any single expense over $25.

## 3. Meal Reimbursement Limits

Meal reimbursement is capped at $75 per day for domestic travel and $100 per day for international travel, inclusive of tips. Alcohol is not a reimbursable expense under any circumstance.

## 4. Travel Booking

All air travel and hotel bookings must be made through the company's approved travel portal. Employees who book outside the approved portal will be reimbursed only up to the amount that would have been incurred through the approved portal.

## 5. Approval Workflow

Expense reports under $500 require manager approval only. Expense reports of $500 or more require both manager and department head approval before reimbursement is processed. Reimbursement is typically issued within 10 business days of final approval.

## 6. Non-Reimbursable Expenses

The following are not reimbursable: personal entertainment, traffic or parking fines, expenses for a spouse or guest, and any expense lacking a valid receipt.
